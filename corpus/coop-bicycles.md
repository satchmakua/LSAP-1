CHAIR (MRS. OKONKWO): The time is 7:04 p.m. I call this emergency meeting of the Larchmere Court Housing Cooperative Board of Directors to order. Present are five of seven directors, which constitutes a quorum. For the record, the emergency item is bicycles in the courtyard. Mr. Farthing is taking the minutes.

FARTHING: I'm taking the minutes.

CHAIR: Thank you. Before we open the floor, I remind everyone that this is a meeting of the board, not a general assembly, and that residents in attendance may speak only when recognized.

VOICE (UNIT 4C): So why'd you email everybody, then.

CHAIR: Because it concerns everybody, Mr. Delgado, but recognition still—

DELGADO (4C): I'm just saying you invited us and now we can't talk.

CHAIR: You may talk when recognized. Mr. Prentiss, you asked for this meeting. Please state the concern.

PRENTISS (2A): Thank you, Madam Chair. As you know, the courtyard is a shared amenity. It is not a parking garage. On Tuesday I counted eleven bicycles chained to the railings, the pear tree, and, in one case, the bench my late wife donated in 2009.

DELGADO (4C): That's my son's bike on the tree. It's a tree. Bikes live outside.

CHAIR: You are not recognized.

DELGADO (4C): Sorry.

PRENTISS (2A): The plaque on the bench, I would note, says "For Quiet Reflection." You cannot reflect quietly with a Huffy leaning on your knee.

FARTHING: Do you want the brand of the bike in the minutes?

PRENTISS (2A): For accuracy.

FARTHING: Huffy.

CHAIR: Mr. Prentiss, do you have a proposed motion?

PRENTISS (2A): I move that all bicycles be removed from the courtyard within seventy-two hours and stored in the basement bicycle room.

DIRECTOR VANCE: Point of order. There is no bicycle room.

PRENTISS (2A): There is a room. In the basement.

VANCE: That is the boiler room, and it has been the boiler room since the Carter administration.

DELGADO (4C): It says "Bicycle Room" on the door.

VANCE: That is a label somebody printed in 1998 on a dot-matrix printer, and I would know, because I printed it, and I was wrong.

CHAIR: Let us stay on the current motion. Is there a second?

DIRECTOR HALLORAN: I'll second it to move things along, but I want it noted I second under protest.

FARTHING: Seconded under protest. Can you protest a second?

CHAIR: We'll clarify later. Discussion.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): I have been a shareholder for thirty-one years and I would like to say that in thirty-one years nobody has ever once returned my casserole dish.

CHAIR: Mrs. Liebowitz, that is not the—

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): It's a Corningware. Cornflower pattern. I brought it to the memorial for Mr. Prentiss's wife with a noodle kugel and it never came home.

PRENTISS (2A): I do not have your dish.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): I'm not saying you have my dish. I'm saying we don't return things in this building. That's the culture. Bikes, dishes, the mail.

CHAIR: We are discussing bicycles.

VANCE: If I may speak to the motion. Removing bicycles in seventy-two hours without a designated storage location simply relocates the problem to the sidewalk, where the city will impound them, and then we are liable.

DELGADO (4C): See? Liable.

HALLORAN: We are not liable for the city.

VANCE: We are liable for the railings. Those railings are prewar wrought iron and the chains are scratching them.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): The railings were repainted in 2014 the wrong green.

HALLORAN: The green was voted on.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): It was voted on at a meeting in August when half the building was in the Berkshires.

CHAIR: The color of the railings is not on tonight's agenda.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Everything is connected, Grace.

CHAIR: Please address the chair as Madam Chair.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): I've known you since you moved in, Grace.

CHAIR: Madam Chair.

DELGADO (4C): Can I be recognized now?

CHAIR: You are recognized, Mr. Delgado, briefly.

DELGADO (4C): My kid rides that bike to school. If it's in the boiler room he can't get it at seven in the morning because the boiler room's locked and Yuri has the key and Yuri doesn't get up till nine.

VANCE: I resent that characterization.

DELGADO (4C): Are you Yuri?

VANCE: I am the acting superintendent liaison and I get up at eight.

FARTHING: Do I record "eight"?

VANCE: Record "before nine."

CHAIR: Mr. Farthing, "before nine."

NEW VOICE (PENNY, 1G): Hi, sorry, I'm new, I moved in in March, unit 1G? I just want to say I actually like the bikes? It feels like people live here.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): People do live here. That's the trouble.

PENNY (1G): I didn't mean—

PRENTISS (2A): The bench, Madam Chair.

CHAIR: We have not forgotten the bench.

HALLORAN: Point of information. When was the bench actually approved? Because I have no memory of a bench vote.

PRENTISS (2A): The bench was a gift. Gifts do not require votes.

HALLORAN: Everything on common property requires a vote. That's in the bylaws.

VANCE: Article Nine.

HALLORAN: Article Nine.

PRENTISS (2A): Are you suggesting my wife's memorial bench is an unauthorized structure?

HALLORAN: I'm suggesting that if we're getting rid of unauthorized objects in the courtyard, the bench is technically—

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Leave the bench out of it, Marty, you monster.

HALLORAN: I am simply applying the standard evenly.

DELGADO (4C): Oh, NOW there's a standard.

CHAIR: Order. Order, please. Mr. Farthing, please note there is cross-talk.

FARTHING: I'm writing "cross-talk."

PRENTISS (2A): This is exactly why I left the board in 2011.

VANCE: You were removed in 2011.

PRENTISS (2A): I resigned.

VANCE: You were removed for the recycling incident and then you framed it as a resignation in the newsletter.

PENNY (1G): What was the recycling incident?

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Oh, honey, don't.

CHAIR: The recycling incident is not on the agenda.

DELGADO (4C): Nothing's on the agenda except it all is.

HALLORAN: Point of order. I move to table the bicycle motion until we establish a lawful storage location.

VANCE: I second.

PRENTISS (2A): You cannot table my motion in favor of a room that does not exist.

CHAIR: A motion to table is not debatable. We will vote. All in favor of tabling—

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Before we vote I would just like it in the minutes that the Corningware was cornflower and I have the lid.

FARTHING: You have the lid but not the dish?

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): The lid came home. The dish did not. Explain that.

FARTHING: I can't put that in the minutes as a question.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Put it in as a fact. Lid: present. Dish: absent.

CHAIR: We are voting on the motion to table. All in favor.

VANCE: Aye.

HALLORAN: Aye.

CHAIR: Opposed.

PRENTISS (2A): Nay, and I want my nay loud.

FARTHING: How do I—

PRENTISS (2A): Capital N.

CHAIR: Director Ferreira, you have not voted.

FERREIRA: I abstain. I've been on mute this whole time and I only just got the door thing to work.

CHAIR: You are physically in the room, Director Ferreira.

FERREIRA: I know, I got confused, I thought this was the Zoom.

CHAIR: The motion to table carries, two to one with one abstention and—Mr. Delgado, you cannot vote, you are not a director.

DELGADO (4C): I was raising my hand to go to the bathroom, it's a reflex.

CHAIR: The motion is tabled. We will reconvene when a storage location has been confirmed by the superintendent liaison, who rises before nine.

VANCE: I resent that this is now my job.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): Welcome to Larchmere.

PENNY (1G): Should I chain my bike somewhere else in the meantime, or—

PRENTISS (2A): Not the bench.

PENNY (1G): Not the bench, got it.

CHAIR: Is there any further business.

HALLORAN: I move we adjourn.

DELGADO (4C): Second.

CHAIR: You can't— fine. Seconded. All in favor. The meeting is adjourned at 7:41 p.m. Mrs. Liebowitz, the lid is not going in the minutes.

MRS. LIEBOWITZ (6B): We'll see.

FARTHING: I'm writing "we'll see."
