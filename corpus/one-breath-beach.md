The metal thing at his throat clicks and falls away and suddenly there is no more weight, no more tug behind the ears, no more line running back to the hand that has held it since the beginning of everything, and the whole of the world tilts open in front of him like a mouth that will never close, wide and white and roaring softly at its far edge, and he does not think but goes, he goes the way water goes downhill, the way a held breath goes out of a chest, front paws thrown forward and hind paws gathering under him and thrown forward again so that for a stretched shining instant all four feet leave the sand at once and he is nothing but a shape flung through salt air, and the sand comes up cool and packed beneath the pads of his feet and then loose and dragging and then packed again where the tide has smoothed it, and it is a surface that answers him, that pushes back, that flings itself away behind him in little cast-up sprays each time he strikes it, and the smell of it is enormous, the smell is a country all its own, rot and brine and dried weed and something dead and glorious somewhere off to the left and the clean cold mineral breath of the water coming steady off the waves, layer on layer of it pouring into him faster than he can name any single thread of it, and he runs straight at the line where the wet dark sand goes silver, straight at the noise, and the noise resolves as he comes into a green wall lifting and curling and folding itself down flat with a boom he feels in the flat bones of his chest, and a skin of foam comes racing up the slope toward him hissing all along its edge, and he skids and wheels and the foam catches his forefeet in a shock of cold that runs all the way up into his shoulders and out along his spine, and he leaps sideways yelping not from fear but from the sheer unbearable brightness of the cold, and he bites at it, snaps at the retreating lace of it, gets a mouthful of froth that tastes of the whole grey heaving depth beyond, and he shakes his head and the world sprays off his ears in a jeweled halo and he is already turning, already pointing himself back up the long empty beach that runs and runs and does not end, that has no fence and no gate and no corner where the good smell stops, and he pours himself into it again.

And now the running is the whole of him, now there is no dog apart from the running, only the ladder of his legs reaching and folding and reaching under the barrel of his ribs that swings and pumps like a bellows, only the hot pink flag of his tongue streaming out the side of his mouth and the wind combing back through the fur along his flanks and lifting the loose skin of his jowls and drying the salt to a fine white crust at the corners of his eyes, and a gull tips up off the sand ahead of him and beats away low and slantwise and he swerves to follow it with a bark torn straight out of his belly, chasing the shadow it drags across the beach more than the bird itself, the shadow that skims the ridged wet sand and flickers over stones and never once lets him close the gap, and he loses it to the sun and does not care, wheels away after a scrap of dried kelp that the wind is tumbling end over end, pounces on it, flings it up, catches it, gallops with it flagging in his teeth until it is nothing and he spits it out, and there is a stick, an actual stick, a whole grey branch worn smooth and heavy, and he seizes it in the middle and carries it high with his neck arched proud as though the empty beach were lined with watchers all the way to the dunes, and the stick is too long and one end drags and trips him and he goes down shoulder first into the soft cool sand and rolls, rolls onto his back with all four feet in the air paddling at the sky, twisting his spine against the delicious grit that works down into the fur, grinding his shoulders and the top of his skull into it, and the sky above him is a single enormous unbroken blue with the sun standing in the middle of it pouring down warmth like a hand laid flat across his belly, and he lies upside down for a moment in a helpless ecstasy of squirming before some new bright thing tugs at him and he flips over and is up and running again before the sand has finished falling from his coat.

And the tide reaches for him and he dances at its edge now, learned and bold, darting in to slap both forepaws down in the shallow sheet of a spent wave and springing back before the cold can climb him, again and again, a game with no rules but the rhythm of the water breathing in and out, and the wet sand takes the round prints of his feet and the next wave licks them smooth so that behind him the beach forgets him as fast as he writes himself upon it, and he splashes out ankle-deep and the small waves knock at his chest and he bites the tops off them and the salt stings sweetly in his throat, and he stands for one second belly-deep in the cold pull of it with the whole grey muscle of the sea sliding around his legs and the horizon lifting and dropping and the wind flattening his ears back and the light shattering into a thousand moving coins across the surface all around him, and it is too much, it is gloriously too much, and he explodes back up the sand shedding water in a long silver rope, and he runs the way he began, flat out, ears streaming, tongue flying, chest heaving, running now purely because the running is joy and the joy is running and there is nothing under the wide bright sky that he wants except more of this and more and more, until at last, somewhere high up the warm dry slope where the sand is pale and soft and holds the whole day's sun stored deep inside it, his legs begin to loosen beneath him and his gallop stumbles down into a lope and the lope folds down into a trot and the trot wobbles and gives, and he lets it give, he lets his front legs buckle and his chest drop and he pours himself down all at once into the warm dry sand that rises up soft to meet him and takes his weight completely, and he lies there with his ribs going like a great warm engine, sides heaving, tongue lolling flat and dark against the pale grains, salt drying tight across his muzzle, one ear cocked to the endless boom of the waves and the whole hot beach humming underneath him, and his eyes half close against the blaze of it, and everything in him is a single long unbroken hum of gladness, and he is done, and he is happy, and he sleeps.
