NOTICE — Posted 3rd June

Would the person or persons who removed the large zucchini (marrow class, plot 14) sometime after Saturday's watering please have the courtesy to return it, or at minimum leave a note of explanation. This was not a surplus vegetable left out for the sharing table. It was labelled. The label read DO NOT TAKE — SHOW ENTRY. I do not wish to make a fuss. I merely wish to understand.

— H. Pargeter, Plot 14, Committee Secretary (Correspondence)

---

RE: NOTICE — Posted 4th June

Harold, no one is going to steal a zucchini. It fell off. They do that when they get to a certain size, the stem gives, gravity finishes the argument. Check under your own leaves before you accuse the whole membership of larceny. Also the sharing table is for everyone and you cannot "label" a thing DO NOT TAKE and then expect the rest of us to feel like criminals every time we walk past your plot, which frankly we already do.

— Someone Who Has Better Things To Do

---

CORRECTION — Posted 4th June (later)

The above unsigned notice is out of order under Section 4(b) of the Allotment Constitution, which requires all noticeboard correspondence to be attributed. I have removed it from the board and pinned it here in the Corrections column, where it belongs, so that the record is complete. I note for the file that the zucchini did not "fall off." I checked under the leaves. I checked under all of them. I have photographs. The stem was cut. Cleanly. With a tool.

— H. Pargeter (Committee Secretary, Correspondence)

---

Posted 5th June

Harold love it was me who took your unsigned notice down and put it back up again three times, I keep tidying and you keep un-tidying, we are going to wear out the drawing pins. But I did want to say I saw a fox near the compost Tuesday and they will carry off all sorts, my nephew lost a whole rabbit hutch door to a fox, well not the fox exactly but you know what I mean. Have you considered a fox.

— Marjorie, Plot 3, Refreshments

---

Posted 5th June

I have considered a fox, Marjorie. A fox does not use a knife. A fox does not sever a stem on the diagonal at a consistent angle of approximately forty degrees. I have measured the remaining stub. I enclose the measurement in the file. A fox, moreover, would have eaten it here, leaving remains. There are no remains. There is only absence. An absence shaped exactly like eleven weeks of my care.

— H. Pargeter

---

Posted 7th June

I would like it noted that yesterday, being watering day, several members were overheard referring to me as "the marrow man" and "him with the tape measure," and one member — I will not name Dennis — said, and I quote as best I recall, "Somebody put that thing out of its misery." I do not think it is too much to expect basic collegial respect while a theft is under active investigation. The zucchini had a name. It was going to be entered in the Regional under the name Goliath. I am not ashamed of that.

— H. Pargeter

---

Posted 7th June

Nobody is investigating anything Harold there is no investigation you have appointed yourself to investigate a courgette. And it is a courgette. Calling it a marrow does not make it a marrow, it makes it a courgette you failed to pick on time. This is the whole problem with you and the show, you leave everything on the plant too long trying to win and then you're surprised when nature or a neighbour intervenes. I did not take it. I want that on the record too since apparently we're all keeping records now.

— Dennis, Plot 9

---

Posted 9th June

To the Growers of This Green Acre —

I write not to accuse but to grieve, and I hope you will forgive a plainer man his one attempt at verse, offered in the spirit of peace:

O green one, swollen in the morning damp,
you rose beneath my hand from seed to lamp,
and I who woke at five to bring you drink
now stand at your bare stem and cannot think.
Was it a knife? A neighbour? Was it spite?
You who were mine when I turned out the light
and gone by dawn — I do not name your thief,
I only lay my sorrow like a leaf
upon this board, and ask the guilty soul:
did winning matter, that you took me whole?

— H. Pargeter, Plot 14

---

Posted 9th June (later)

Harold that made me cry a bit by the runner beans I don't mind saying. I have put the kettle on if you want to come up.

— Marjorie, Plot 3

---

Posted 10th June

Formal complaint. The above poem constitutes, in my view, an accusation dressed as a lament, and the line "Was it a neighbour? Was it spite?" can only be read as directed at those of us on adjacent plots, i.e. me, Plot 9, being the nearest neighbour and the one Harold "will not name" while naming twice. I request the Committee convene to address defamation by poetry. I have not taken his courgette. I would not know where to put a thing that size.

— Dennis, Plot 9

---

RULING — Posted 11th June

As Committee Secretary (Correspondence) I have reviewed the complaint of Plot 9 and find that a poem, being an expression of feeling, cannot defame, per no precedent I could locate but common sense. Complaint dismissed. I remind all members that the Regional Show closes for entries on the 20th and that I now have nothing to enter, a fact I set down without comment.

— H. Pargeter (Committee Secretary, Correspondence)

---

Posted 14th June

I have thought about whether to write this for a week and I am going to write it. It was me. I took the courgette. I want to say marrow because I know that is what Harold wanted it to be, so I will say marrow. I took the marrow.

I did not do it out of spite and I did not do it to win, I have never entered anything and I never will. I did it because on the Friday night I sat on the bench between our plots after everyone had gone and I looked at that thing Harold had grown, that enormous ridiculous green thing he'd measured every single day and covered with a towel on cold nights, I've seen him, we've all seen him, and I thought: nobody has ever in my whole life wanted anything about me the way that man wants that vegetable to be big. And I couldn't stand to look at it another minute. So I cut it. Diagonally, I don't know why, my hands were shaking. And I took it home and I could not eat it and I could not throw it away so it is in my shed, Plot 22, under the deckchair, and it has gone soft now and it smells and I am so sorry Harold, I am sorrier than the courgette deserves and less sorry than you do.

— Eileen, Plot 22, Membership (Lapsed)

---

Posted 15th June

Eileen. Come up for tea. Marjorie has the kettle on more or less permanently now. There is nothing to forgive that a cup won't cover. And it was a marrow. You said so yourself and I am inclined, for once, to accept the testimony of the party in the best position to know.

I will not be entering the Regional this year. I find I do not want to.

— H. Pargeter, Plot 14

---

CORRECTION — Posted 15th June (later)

The Committee wishes to note, for completeness of the file, that this matter is now closed, and that Dennis is owed an apology, which is hereby entered: Dennis, I am sorry. It was never you. It was somebody who wanted to be wanted, and I of all people should have recognised the symptom sooner.

— H. Pargeter (Committee Secretary, Correspondence)
