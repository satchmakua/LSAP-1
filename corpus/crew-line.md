"Ordering," Marco called from the pass. "Two scallop, one duck, one duck no jus, and a bisque behind."

"Two scallop, heard," said Renny, already reaching into the low fridge.

"Duck, duck, one dry, chef, yes." That was Otis on the meat station, and he tapped the board twice with the flat of his knife the way he always did.

"Bisque up in ninety," from the far end, from Priya, who never looked up when she said a time and was never wrong about one.

"Somebody give me a clean towel that's actually clean," said Renny.

"Define clean," Otis said.

"Not that towel. That towel has seen things."

"That towel has a family."

"Ordering," Marco said again, cutting through. "Table twelve, all day now that's six scallop, three duck, two dry, one bisque, one soup no cream, and hold me one halibut for the window."

"Six scallop all day," Renny said. "I got four in, two coming."

"Three duck all day, two dry," Otis said. "Fire the halibut?"

"Not yet. Hold it."

"Holding a fish is a sad life, chef."

"Priya, where's my bisque?"

"Coming up now, coming up, hands." She slid two bowls onto the pass and Marco wiped the rims with a thumb and a paper without appearing to look.

"Hands!" he shouted, and a runner ghosted up out of nowhere, took the bowls, and was gone.

"Where do they come from," Renny said, plating scallops with the little bronzed sides up. "You never see them arrive. Like weather."

"Ordering," Marco said. "Two more twelve-tops just walked, so buckle it. Fourteen is fired all at once, they want it together, four duck, four scallop, two bisque."

"Four duck," Otis said, and then, lower, "four duck, four duck, on the same tick, right, okay," talking himself into the pans.

"You good?"

"I'm gorgeous. Renny, I need the pass in two, don't crowd me."

"I'm not crowding you, I'm existing near you."

"That's crowding."

"Behind, hot, behind," Priya said, sweeping between them with a rondeau, and both men leaned without looking, a little dance they'd done ten thousand times.

"Scallop's resting," Renny said. "Marco, scallop wants to go, it won't hold."

"Then it goes. Where's the duck."

"Duck needs a minute," Otis said. "Duck is a proud animal, it doesn't get rushed."

"The proud animal is dead, Otis."

"Doesn't mean it lost its dignity."

"Ninety seconds on duck," Otis said, which was him giving in.

"Hands!" Marco called, and the runner appeared and the scallops were gone. "Ordering. Nine walked in the door, chef says they're friends of the owner."

A groan traveled the length of the line like a wave, low to high.

"Friends of the owner," Renny said. "My favorite words. Right up there with 'quick question' and 'is the kitchen still open.'"

"They want the tasting, all nine, and one's allergic to shellfish, one's vegetarian, and one, and I quote, 'doesn't do textures.'"

"Doesn't do textures," Priya repeated flatly. "Everything has a texture. Air has a texture."

"Tell air I said what's up," Otis said.

"Nine tasting, two mods, plating the tasting whenever you're ready but I need the vegetarian to walk with the table so nobody sits there feeling like a garnish."

"Copy the veg walks with," Renny said. "I got the veg, I'll build it. Priya, you got a mushroom thing that isn't sad?"

"Everything I make sings, Renny."

"Sing at me later, I need it in six minutes."

"Six," Priya said, that flat certainty again.

"Ordering," Marco said, relentless now, the tickets curling up out of the printer faster than a hand could tear them. "Table eight is a re-fire, they said the duck was cold."

Silence, brief, dangerous.

"The duck," Otis said, very carefully, "was not cold."

"I know."

"The duck was correct. The duck was a religious experience."

"I know, Otis. Re-fire the duck."

"I'll re-fire it," Otis said, "but I want it noted that I am doing so under protest."

"It's noted. It's in the book. 'Otis protests.' Fire it."

"Firing under protest," Otis said, and slapped a new breast down and the fat sang.

"Renny, you got a hand for me for a plate of two on nine?"

"I got a hand, gimme two seconds, I'm nose-deep in mushroom right now."

"Priya said six on the mushroom, you're not nose-deep, you're anticipating."

"I contain multitudes."

"Contain them faster."

"Hot behind, corner, corner," a runner said, learning the words, saying them a beat too late.

"Say it before you move, love, not after," Priya said, not unkindly.

"Sorry chef, corner!"

"Better. Now you're weather too."

"Ordering," Marco said. "This is the wall. This is the top of the hill, people, everything landed at once and we ride it now. All day I have: fourteen scallop, eleven duck, four dry, six bisque, three soup, the nine tasting, two vegetarian, one no-textures, and a partridge in a pear tree."

"We doing partridge now?" Otis said. "Since when do we have partridge?"

"It's a figure of speech."

"Because I'll cook a partridge. I'll cook the pear tree."

"Fourteen scallop, heard, oh my god, fourteen," Renny said, and you could hear the smile clenched in his teeth. "Marco, I got sear space for eight at a time, tell twelve their scallops love them but they need to be patient."

"Twelve is being patient. Twelve is drinking. Twelve is fine."

"Bless twelve. Twelve is a good table."

"Bisque, two hands," Priya said, and slid them up.

"Hands! Where are my hands!"

"Right here chef," two of them now, the ghosts multiplying under pressure, and the bowls went and the plates went and the pass was clean again for one whole second before it filled.

"Renny, wipe. There's a smear."

"There is no smear."

"Bottom left, it's looking at me."

"It's the light."

"Wipe the light, then."

Renny wiped it. There had been a smear.

"Duck up," Otis said. "Both. Re-fire and the fourteen-top duck. And I'd like everyone to observe that this re-fire is objectively better than the first one, which was already perfect, so table eight has now been rewarded for lying."

"We don't reward liars in this kitchen," Marco said, dressing the plate.

"We absolutely do, we do it forty times a night."

"Fair. Hands!"

"How we doing on the tasting," Priya asked, over her shoulder, plating something small and green with tweezers, of all things, tweezers, in the middle of the storm.

"Tasting's coming together, I need the no-textures thing to actually have no textures, which is a paradox I'm choosing not to think about," Renny said.

"Give them a soup."

"Soup has a texture."

"Give them water."

"Water has —"

"Don't," Renny said. "Do not finish that."

"Ordering," Marco said, and then, a beat later, softer, the first soft thing he'd said in an hour: "and that might be the top of it. Printer's slowing. We might be over the hump."

"Don't say that," three voices at once.

"You'll jinx it," Otis said. "You said the word 'slow' and now nine more are walking in, watch, I can feel it, I feel it in my knees."

"Your knees are always wrong."

"My knees predicted the Tuesday rush."

"Everybody predicted the Tuesday rush, it was on the reservation book."

The printer chattered, spat, and went still. Genuinely still. The line held its breath in the way a line does, all four of them frozen a half-second over their stations, listening to the quiet like it was a rumor.

"Okay," Marco said. "Okay. We are through the wall. Renny, finish the tasting. Otis, breathe. Priya —"

"Six minutes," Priya said, though nobody had asked her anything, and put the tweezers down, and cracked her neck, and picked them up again.

"Hands," Marco said, almost gently, and the last plates of the crush went out into the light, and someone, nobody ever knew who, started to whistle.
