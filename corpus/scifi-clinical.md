At 03:42 station time, Technician Reyes recorded the first entry in the anomaly log. The entry noted a deviation of 0.4 kelvin in the coolant loop serving Module C, sensor array 14. The nominal operating temperature for the loop was 277.2 kelvin. The recorded value was 277.6 kelvin. Reyes cross-referenced the reading against the redundant sensor, array 15, which reported 277.3 kelvin. The discrepancy between the two arrays was 0.3 kelvin, exceeding the calibration tolerance of 0.1 kelvin.

Reyes was assigned to the third maintenance shift, a rotation that ran from 00:00 to 08:00 station time. The shift covered four modules and the interconnecting corridor. During the third shift, station occupancy was reduced to a skeleton crew of six, of whom four were asleep in the habitation ring. The two waking personnel were Reyes and Communications Officer Adeyemi, who was stationed in the operations node approximately forty meters from Module C along the primary corridor.

The station orbited at an altitude of 412 kilometers. The orbital period was 92.6 minutes. At the time of the first log entry, the station was in the eclipse portion of its orbit, meaning it was on the night side of the planet and receiving no direct solar radiation. During eclipse, the external radiators dissipated stored thermal load, and the coolant loops typically registered a gradual decline in temperature. The recorded increase in loop temperature was therefore inconsistent with the expected thermal behavior during eclipse.

Reyes initiated the standard diagnostic sequence. The sequence consisted of eleven checks, executed in a fixed order. The first check confirmed that the coolant pump serving Module C was operating within specification. The pump reported a flow rate of 2.1 liters per minute against a nominal rate of 2.0 to 2.2 liters per minute. The second check confirmed the integrity of the loop by measuring pressure at three points. The pressures were 340, 338, and 341 kilopascals, all within the acceptable band of 330 to 350 kilopascals. The third through seventh checks confirmed the operation of the valves, the heat exchanger, and the accumulator. No deviation was found.

The eighth check queried the sensor arrays for error flags. Array 14 reported no error flag. Array 15 reported no error flag. Both arrays had passed their most recent scheduled calibration, conducted eleven days prior. The ninth check compared the current readings against the historical record for the preceding thirty orbits. The historical record showed that during eclipse the loop temperature had declined at an average rate of 0.02 kelvin per minute. In the twelve minutes preceding the first log entry, the loop temperature had increased at a rate of 0.033 kelvin per minute. The direction of the temperature change was opposite to the historical pattern.

Reyes recorded the results of the diagnostic sequence in the anomaly log at 04:07. The entry stated that the coolant loop serving Module C was registering an anomalous temperature increase during eclipse, that the increase was not attributable to pump failure, loop breach, valve malfunction, or sensor error flag, and that the source of the additional thermal load had not been identified. Reyes classified the anomaly as Priority 3, which required continued monitoring but not immediate escalation to the sleeping crew.

At 04:11, Reyes physically entered Module C. Module C contained the station's biological sample storage and two experiment racks. The module was pressurized and maintained at 294 kelvin ambient. Reyes used a handheld infrared thermometer to survey the surfaces of the equipment. The experiment racks registered 293 to 295 kelvin. The sample storage unit registered 277 kelvin at its external casing, consistent with its refrigeration setpoint. Reyes traced the coolant line along the module wall and measured its surface temperature at four points. The measurements were 278.1, 278.4, 279.0, and 279.2 kelvin, increasing along the direction of coolant flow toward the heat exchanger. The increase in surface temperature along the line indicated that heat was being added to the coolant somewhere within Module C.

Reyes surveyed the module for equipment that could contribute thermal load. The two experiment racks were powered. Rack A drew 340 watts. Rack B drew 355 watts. Both values were within their operating specifications. The sample storage unit drew 210 watts. The lighting and environmental systems drew a combined 180 watts. Reyes summed the module's power consumption and compared it to the value reported by the station's power management system. The power management system reported a module draw of 1,340 watts. The sum of the individually metered loads was 1,085 watts. The difference was 255 watts. The 255-watt difference was unaccounted for.

Reyes recorded the power discrepancy in the anomaly log at 04:29. The entry noted that Module C was drawing 255 watts more than the sum of its metered loads and that the excess draw was a plausible source of the anomalous thermal load in the coolant loop, since 255 watts of dissipated electrical power was consistent in magnitude with a temperature rise of the observed rate given the loop's thermal mass and flow rate. Reyes did not identify the device or circuit responsible for the excess draw.

At 04:35, Reyes contacted Adeyemi over the internal communication channel and requested a review of the power distribution telemetry from the operations node. Adeyemi confirmed the module draw of 1,340 watts and confirmed that the draw had begun to increase at approximately 03:30 station time. Adeyemi stated that the increase had been gradual, rising from 1,085 watts to 1,340 watts over a period of roughly fifty minutes, and that no alarm had been generated because the total remained below the module's rated capacity of 2,000 watts. Adeyemi noted that the onset of the power increase preceded the first temperature deviation by approximately twelve minutes.

Reyes conducted a circuit-by-circuit survey of Module C using the panel-mounted current meters. The survey identified eleven circuits. Ten circuits reported currents consistent with their known loads. The eleventh circuit, designated C-11, was labeled in the station schematic as an unassigned spare. Circuit C-11 reported a current of 1.06 amperes at 240 volts, corresponding to a draw of 254 watts. According to the station configuration record, no equipment was connected to circuit C-11. Reyes measured the surface temperature of the C-11 junction box. The junction box registered 331 kelvin.

Reyes recorded the finding in the anomaly log at 04:52. The entry stated that circuit C-11, listed as unassigned in the configuration record, was drawing 254 watts, that the draw accounted for the previously unexplained excess consumption and the anomalous thermal load, and that the destination of the current within the module had not yet been determined. Reyes upgraded the anomaly classification from Priority 3 to Priority 2, which required notification of the station commander at the start of the next waking shift.

At 04:58, Reyes traced the physical routing of circuit C-11 from the junction box. The cable ran along the module wall behind the sample storage unit and terminated at a sealed enclosure mounted to the module's aft bulkhead. The enclosure was not listed in the station configuration record. The enclosure measured approximately 30 by 20 by 15 centimeters. Its external surface temperature was 342 kelvin. The enclosure bore no manufacturer label, serial number, or identifying mark. It was fastened to the bulkhead with six bolts of a pattern not used elsewhere on the station.

Reyes photographed the enclosure and appended the images to the anomaly log at 05:06. The final entry of the shift stated that an unlisted powered enclosure had been located in Module C, that it was the source of the anomalous thermal load, and that its function was unknown. Reyes did not attempt to open the enclosure. The coolant loop temperature at the close of the entry was 278.1 kelvin and continued to rise at a rate of 0.031 kelvin per minute. Reyes remained in Module C and continued to monitor the readings until the end of the shift at 08:00.
