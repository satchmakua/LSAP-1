The archive is housed in a building that does not appear on any survey, and yet its address is recorded within the archive itself, on a card that describes the building as containing the archive that contains the card. One learns to accept such statements early, or one does not remain. The condition of admission is not a signature but a tolerance, a willingness to treat contradiction as a species of furniture: something to sit upon rather than resolve.

Consider the catalogue. Every archive requires a catalogue, an index of its holdings, a smaller thing that points toward the larger. Here the catalogue is complete, which is to say it lists every document the archive holds. Because the catalogue is a document the archive holds, the catalogue lists itself. This is unremarkable; many catalogues do. What is less remarkable and more troubling is that the catalogue's own entry, in listing the catalogue, must describe its contents, which include the entry describing the catalogue, which includes the description of that description. The entry is therefore infinitely long, and yet it occupies a single card of ordinary dimensions, filed in the ordinary way, retrievable in an afternoon. One reads it and finds it finite. One reads it again and finds it has grown, though nothing has been added. The growth is not physical. It is a property of attention, the way a corridor lengthens when you count your steps along it.

I have spent what I take to be years in these rooms, though the archive holds a document attesting that I arrived this morning, and another attesting that I have not yet arrived, and both are stamped with seals I recognize as my own, in an ink I do not remember mixing. The archive is not deceptive. Deception requires an intention to mislead, and the archive intends nothing; it merely records, and among the things it records is the fact of its own recording, and among those facts is the recording of that fact, so that the archive is not so much a collection of documents as a document collecting itself, folding along a seam that has no edge.

The clerks, if they are clerks, do not speak of paradox. They speak of coverage. An archive is said to have coverage when it holds a record of a given event. This archive aspires to total coverage, which would require it to hold a record of every event, including the events of its own holding, including the event of its aspiration toward total coverage, including this present sentence, which is now, I am given to understand, filed under a heading I am not permitted to read, because reading it would constitute an event requiring a record, which record would require reading, and so on into a regress that the archive has already, somewhere, patiently completed. Completion here is not a terminus. It is a texture. The infinite regress does not fail to arrive; it arrives everywhere at once and then continues arriving, the way a mirror facing a mirror does not eventually fill with images but is already full, from the first instant, of a fullness that keeps deepening without ever growing more full.

There is a room, or a card describing a room, in which the entire archive is said to be contained. This is the paradox stated plainly, and it is the paradox the institution is built to metabolize. A thing cannot contain itself, we are taught, as a box cannot be placed inside the box it is. And yet the room is small, smaller than the building, and within it stands a model of the building, and within the model a smaller room, and within that a smaller model, and the descent does not stop where the eye stops, for the archive holds documentation of the further terms, each attested, each cross-referenced, each pointing to the term beneath it and to the term above, so that the containment is not spatial but referential. The archive contains itself not by swallowing but by naming. To name a thing completely is to hold it. The archive names itself completely. Therefore the archive holds itself, and the holding is one more thing it names, and the naming is one more thing it holds.

I want to be precise, because precision is the only comfort available and the only comfort the situation deserves. The impossibility is not that a large thing fits inside a small thing. Impossibilities of scale are trivial and can be waved away with the word *representation*. The impossibility is that the representation is exhaustive. A map that included itself would ordinarily fail at the second iteration, blurring into a smudge too small to render its own detail. This archive does not blur. Each iteration is as sharp as the first. I have descended forty-one levels of nested description and found the forty-first as legible as the surface, the ink as wet, the seals as fresh, and I understood, coldly, without alarm, that legibility here is not a resource that depletes. It is a rule of the space, like gravity, and the rule is that no description ever exhausts itself into vagueness, because vagueness is the one thing the archive does not hold, having no document that says *and here the detail gives out.*

What, then, is missing? An archive is defined as much by its lacunae as by its coverage, and I confess I searched for the gap, the empty folder, the citation without a source. I did not find one. There is a card that reads: *There is no document this archive lacks.* Because this card is a document, and it asserts a complete lack of lack, it forbids its own absence, and so it must exist, and so it does. I held it. I set it down. When I looked again the card read: *Except this one,* and I understood that the archive had corrected itself, had found the single flaw required to be consistent, the one lacuna that permits the whole to cohere—the statement that cannot be included, which is included as the statement that cannot be. The archive is complete by being incomplete in exactly one place, and that place moves, and the movement is documented, and the documentation is complete but for one place.

I do not know whether I am a reader of the archive or an entry in it. The distinction assumed a boundary between the observer and the observed, and the archive, holding both, holds the boundary too, and having held it, may relocate it. There is a folder bearing my designation. It contains a description of a person reading a folder bearing their designation, and the description is accurate to the smallest tremor of the hand, including the tremor with which I now close it. I do not tremble. The voice one uses in this place cannot afford to tremble; the tremor would be recorded, and the record would outlast the feeling, and the feeling, seeing itself recorded, would learn it was permanent, and permanence of feeling is unbearable, so we do not feel. We describe. Describing is the local form of survival, and survival is one more document, filed under a heading that describes the filing, in a room that contains the building, in an archive that contains itself, on a card whose address does not appear on any survey, and yet is recorded here, exactly here, where you are reading it now.
