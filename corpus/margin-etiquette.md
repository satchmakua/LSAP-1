Upon entering the drawing room, the well-mannered young person waits to be presented. One does not announce oneself. To thrust one's name upon a gathering is the mark of a household that has failed in its most elementary duties.

*or a household with a doorbell. we have a doorbell, Nana. it does the announcing.*

The gloves are to be removed before one accepts refreshment, and never at table. A glove left upon the hand while dining suggests either a wound the guest is too proud to disclose or an intention to depart with the silver.

*so which am I supposed to be worried about, that they think I'm bleeding or that they think I'm a thief. because those seem like different problems*

*also who brings gloves*

The correct posture at rest is achieved without the support of furniture. The spine carries itself. To lean is to inform the room that one's own frame is insufficient to the occasion, and the occasion will draw its conclusions accordingly.

*the occasion is Great-Aunt Miriam and a plate of the biscuits nobody eats. the occasion has drawn its conclusions about me already and they are not flattering and I've never even leaned in front of it*

*"the spine carries itself." my spine carries a backpack full of textbooks up three flights every day, the spine is FINE, the spine has done its part*

Conversation should be conducted at a volume audible to one's immediate neighbour and to no one beyond. To be overheard from across the room is a discourtesy to every guest between; one's voice becomes a piece of furniture others must walk around.

*this is actually kind of good*
*don't tell anyone I said that*
*I mean it's dumb but the walking-around-your-voice thing is a little bit good*

Topics unsuitable for general conversation include money, illness, religion, and the failings of persons not present.

*so what's LEFT*
*genuinely. money no, sick people no, God no, gossip no. that's the whole menu. that's four out of the five food groups of talking. what am I supposed to do, describe the weather to a woman who has personally survived it for eighty-six years*

The young person does not initiate a subject but attends to those raised by their elders, offering agreement where agreement is warranted and a graceful demurral where it is not. Contradiction, when unavoidable, is softened into inquiry. One does not say, "You are mistaken." One says, "I had understood the matter differently — perhaps you can correct me."

*ohhh. okay. that one I'm stealing. "perhaps you can correct me" while I am in fact correcting THEM. that is diabolical. that's the kind of thing you'd get away with your whole life and no one could touch you. old people have been quietly winning arguments this way for centuries and never told us*

When tea is offered, one accepts the cup with the right hand and does not stir with vigour. The spoon touches the sides of the cup as little as possible. A guest who clatters is a guest whose interior life may be presumed to clatter also.

*the spoon has opinions about my SOUL now*
*"whose interior life may be presumed to clatter also" — my interior life is a group chat and forty open browser tabs and a song I can't get out of my head, so yes, accurate, my interior life clatters, put it on the report card*

Should one be served a dish that is not to one's taste, one consumes a small portion regardless and passes no remark. The host's table is not a subject for review.

*this is the one that's going to break me. she makes the fish thing. the grey fish thing in the grey sauce. I have to eat the grey fish thing and pass no remark and carry my spine and not clatter my soul-spoon and NOT ask her about her hip which is the only thing she wants to talk about anyway*

*wait*
*is the hip "illness" or is the hip just Miriam. is Miriam's hip a forbidden topic or is it the ONLY safe topic because it's hers and she raised it. the manual didn't cover this. the manual has a hole in it exactly the size of Great-Aunt Miriam's hip*

Compliments should be specific and sincere. To praise everything is to praise nothing; the guest who admires all is understood to admire nothing in particular, least of all their host, whom flattery insults far more than honest silence.

One offers to assist in the clearing of the table but accepts the host's refusal on the first or second occasion of its being given. To insist beyond this is to suggest the household is understaffed, an implication no host forgives.

*or you could just help. you could just carry the plates. she lives alone, the "household" is her and a cat that hates everyone equally, there is no staff to be understaffed, there is a small tired woman and a lot of grey fish and I have two working arms*

Upon departure, the guest thanks the host before withdrawing, and does not prolong the leave-taking. A farewell that will not end becomes a second visit for which no one has prepared, and the goodwill of the first is spent in the corridor.
