Minutes of the Emergency Meeting of the Nettleford Parish Committee, held in the Village Hall at 7.30 p.m. on the fourth of March.

Present: Mr H. Dowsett (Chairman), Mrs P. Larkin (Secretary), Mr R. Coombes, Mrs J. Ainsworth, Mr D. Prentice, and Miss E. Whitlow. Apologies were received from the Reverend T. Fossett, who was attending to a christening, and from Mr G. Barlow, who did not state a reason.

1. Opening of the Meeting

1.1 The Chairman opened the meeting at 7.34 p.m., four minutes later than advertised, and apologised for the delay. He explained that he had been unable to reach the meeting by the usual route, the usual route passing directly in front of the post office.

1.2 The minutes of the previous meeting were approved without amendment. Mrs Larkin noted for the record that the previous meeting had concerned the matter of the recycling bins, and that this matter remained unresolved.

2. Statement of the Emergency

2.1 The Chairman stated that the committee had been convened to consider the situation at the post office.

2.2 For the benefit of any member who had not passed the post office that day, the Chairman summarised the situation as follows: a swan had entered the post office, and had not left.

2.3 Mr Coombes asked when the swan had entered. Mrs Larkin, referring to notes provided by the postmistress, Mrs Handley, stated that the swan had entered at approximately 9.15 a.m., shortly after opening, and that it had been present for a period of some ten hours.

2.4 Mr Prentice asked whether the swan had transacted any business. Mrs Larkin replied that it had not.

3. Report from the Postmistress

3.1 Mrs Handley was not present, being unwilling to leave the post office in its current state, but had submitted a written report.

3.2 The report stated that the swan had entered through the propped front door, had proceeded past the queue, and had positioned itself behind the counter in the area reserved for staff.

3.3 The report further stated that the swan had remained largely stationary but had, on three occasions, hissed. The occasions were recorded as follows: at 10.20 a.m., when Mrs Handley attempted to reach the stamps; at 1.05 p.m., when Mrs Handley attempted to reach the pension ledger; and at 3.40 p.m., for no reason that Mrs Handley could identify.

3.4 The report concluded that the post office had conducted no business after 9.15 a.m., with the exception of one customer, Mr Barlow, who had posted a parcel by placing it near the door and departing quickly.

3.5 The committee noted that this accounted for Mr Barlow's absence and for his failure to state a reason.

4. Discussion of the Nature of the Occupation

4.1 Mrs Ainsworth asked whether the swan could properly be described as having occupied the post office, or whether it was merely present within it.

4.2 A discussion followed on the distinction between occupation and presence. Mr Coombes argued that occupation implied intent, and that the intent of the swan could not be established. Mrs Ainsworth argued that ten hours behind the counter constituted occupation regardless of intent.

4.3 The Chairman ruled that, for the purposes of the minutes, the swan would be described as having occupied the post office, without prejudice to the question of intent.

4.4 Miss Whitlow asked whether the swan was aware that it was in a post office. No member was able to answer this question.

5. Consideration of Available Remedies

5.1 The committee moved to consider what might be done.

5.2 Mr Prentice proposed that the swan be asked to leave. The Chairman inquired who would ask it. Mr Prentice did not volunteer. The proposal lapsed for want of a proposer willing to be present.

5.3 Mrs Ainsworth proposed that food be placed outside the post office in order to draw the swan out. Mr Coombes asked what swans ate. A discussion followed. It was generally agreed that bread was traditional but that bread was understood to be harmful to swans. No member could state with confidence what would be both attractive to the swan and not harmful to it.

5.4 Mrs Ainsworth withdrew her proposal on the grounds that the committee did not wish to be responsible for the health of the swan in addition to its removal.

5.5 Miss Whitlow proposed that the matter be referred to a body with expertise in swans. The Chairman asked whether such a body existed. Miss Whitlow believed that it did but could not name it.

5.6 Mr Coombes stated that swans were the property of the Crown. A discussion followed as to whether the committee ought therefore to contact the Crown. The Chairman observed that the committee did not possess a means of contacting the Crown.

5.7 Mrs Larkin noted, for the record, that the matter had now been under discussion for thirty-five minutes and that no remedy had been agreed.

6. The Question of the Fire Brigade

6.1 Mr Prentice raised the possibility of contacting the fire brigade.

6.2 The Chairman asked on what grounds the fire brigade would be contacted, there being no fire.

6.3 Mr Prentice stated that the fire brigade had, in the past, attended to animals in difficulty. Mrs Ainsworth observed that the swan was not in difficulty, and that if any party was in difficulty it was Mrs Handley.

6.4 It was agreed not to contact the fire brigade at this time, but to hold the fire brigade in reserve.

7. Interim Measures at the Post Office

7.1 The committee considered the operation of postal services during the occupation.

7.2 Mrs Larkin reported that Mrs Handley had established a provisional arrangement whereby customers deposited items on a chair inside the door and Mrs Handley retrieved them once the swan appeared settled.

7.3 The committee expressed its appreciation of Mrs Handley's initiative. It was agreed that a letter of thanks would be sent to Mrs Handley, care of the post office, and that the letter would be posted through the ordinary channel, which was the same chair.

7.4 Mr Coombes asked whether the postal service could be considered operational. The Chairman ruled that it could be considered operational in a limited sense.

8. Any Other Business

8.1 The Chairman invited any other business.

8.2 Mrs Ainsworth reported that a second swan had been observed on the green at approximately 6 p.m.

8.3 The committee noted this report. No member proposed any action, and no discussion followed, the significance of the report being generally understood.

9. Date of Next Meeting

9.1 The Chairman proposed that the committee reconvene in one week to review the situation.

9.2 Mrs Larkin asked where the committee would meet if the situation had by then extended beyond the post office. The Chairman replied that the committee would meet in the Village Hall, as usual, and that the committee would consider the extension of the situation as a separate item should it arise.

9.3 The date of the next meeting was fixed for the eleventh of March at 7.30 p.m.

10. Close

10.1 The Chairman closed the meeting at 8.41 p.m.

10.2 Mrs Larkin recorded that, at the time of closing, the swan remained in occupation of the post office.

10.3 Members departed by the rear door of the Village Hall.
