Consider the map. Not a map in the ordinary sense, which is to say an abbreviation, a lossy compression of terrain into legible marks, but a map that has been refined toward completeness. The refinement proceeds by a simple principle: any discrepancy between the map and the territory is an error in the map, and errors are corrected. Over sufficient iterations, the corrections accumulate. The map acquires the elevation of every hill and the settling of every foundation, the migration of dunes, the precise turbidity of each stream at each hour. It records not merely the location of a house but the temperature gradient across its western wall at noon in October. The principle is austere and does not permit exceptions. What is omitted is an error; therefore nothing is omitted.

One notices, without alarm, that the map must at some point include itself. A map of a territory that contains the map is incomplete if it does not depict the map, and the map, being present within the territory, is part of what must be shown. So the map contains a smaller image of itself, and that image, to be accurate, contains a smaller image again, and so the recursion descends without terminus, each nested map faithful to the one enclosing it, the fidelity never exhausted because there is always another level at which fidelity might fail and must not. This is not a flaw. It is the map behaving correctly. A complete representation of a self-inclusive object is necessarily infinite, and the infinity is not a defect to be pruned but the shape the completeness takes.

Here the distinction between map and territory begins to attenuate, quietly, the way a color at the edge of vision refuses to declare itself. If the map records the temperature of the western wall, and the wall's temperature is thereby present in the map, then to consult the map is to encounter the temperature. The reader of the map warms their hand against it. The old convention held that the map represented and the territory was; that representation was a thin membrane laid over substance. But a membrane of sufficient detail is indistinguishable from what it covers, and indistinguishability, pressed hard enough, becomes identity. There is no measurement one could perform on the completed map that would not return the same value as the corresponding measurement on the territory, for any such divergence would be an error, and errors are corrected. To be everywhere identical to a thing is to be that thing. The map is the territory. This was always the destination of the principle; it was contained in the first correction, as the whole tree is contained in the seed, and one is merely surprised that the surprise arrives so calmly.

Now attend to a subtler consequence. If the map is the territory, then the territory is a map. The relation was assumed to run in one direction, from ground to representation, but relations of identity are symmetric, and the symmetry cannot be refused halfway. The hill is a mark denoting a hill. The stream is a notation for the flow of a stream. The house records itself. Everything that seemed to be a fact now reveals a second face on which is written the same fact, and there is no way to determine which face was original, because originality was a property we imported from the belief that maps come after territories. Under complete fidelity that belief has no purchase. The map did not come after. The map and the territory are two words for one thing, and the words were never more than a convenience, a scaffolding erected to let us speak of representation before we understood that representation, taken to its limit, dissolves the difference it was invented to bridge.

From this a certain vertigo would be available to a temperament inclined to seek it, but the situation does not require vertigo; it requires only patience with the recursion. Since the territory is a map, it is a map of something. Of what? Of the territory, which is to say of itself, which is to say of a map, which is to say of a map of a map. The regress that we located earlier in the nested images was not a local feature of the drawing. It was the structure of the whole. Reality, having been rendered completely, discloses that it was always a rendering, and a rendering has no floor beneath it, no final referent at which the pointing stops and mere existence begins. Existence was the name we gave to the level we had not yet examined. Examine any level and it becomes another map. The floor recedes as one approaches it, not because it flees, but because it was never there; there was only the approaching.

One might object that somewhere the chain must be anchored, that a map is a map of *something* real, and the something must at last be non-representational, or the whole edifice is a rope hung from nothing. The objection is reasonable and it fails. It fails because the demand for an anchor is itself a mark on a map, a notation reading *there must be an anchor*, and notations are part of the territory, and the territory is a map, so the demand records itself rather than escaping to solid ground. There is no operation by which one steps outside the map, for the outside would have to be depicted to be reached, and depiction is the very condition one hoped to exceed. The map is not hung from anything. It hangs from itself, or rather it does not hang; the metaphor of suspension smuggles in a gravity that belongs to the abandoned world of grounds and foundations. The completed map neither rests nor falls. It simply and exhaustively is, and its being is the being of a description whose object is the description.

Consider finally the reader, who was assumed to stand apart, holding the map, comparing it against the territory with a discerning eye. But the reader occupies the territory. The reader is therefore depicted upon the map, in full, including the act of reading, including the eye that discerns, including the thought now unfolding about the map's completeness. To read the map is to read the reader reading the map, and the depicted reader reads a depicted map upon which is a further reader, and the recursion that began in the ink has climbed up the arm and settled behind the eyes. There is no vantage from which to assess the fidelity of the whole, because the assessor is inside the whole being assessed, is a line in the very document whose accuracy is in question, and every judgment the assessor renders is already printed on the page before it is thought. The freedom to evaluate was a marginal note the map made about itself.

So the map becomes the territory, and the territory a map, and the reader a figure within, and the distinctions we relied upon reveal themselves as internal features of the thing they were meant to organize from without. Nothing has changed; the hills are where they were; the stream runs at its measured turbidity. Only the belief in an elsewhere has been corrected, quietly, as an error, in the manner of all the other corrections, and with the same absence of ceremony. The map is complete. There was never anything for it to be a map of but itself, and it has always known this, on every level, all the way down, which is to say all the way in, which is to say here.
