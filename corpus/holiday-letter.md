"Okay, everybody, focus. 'Dear friends and family—'"

"Too formal."

"It's a holiday letter, Marcus, not a ransom note. What do you want, 'Yo'?"

"'Yo' is warmer."

"'Dear friends and family, what a year it's been!'"

"No exclamation point."

"You can't open with 'what a year it's been,' that's what people say when someone died."

"Someone almost did. Dad's kidney—"

"Dad's kidney is not going in the letter."

"It's the biggest thing that happened to this family!"

"To Dad. To Dad's kidney. Not to—"

"He'd want it in."

"He would not, he told me specifically—"

"They are famously related, Denise."

"We did not."

"We're literally at the same table right now."

"For the first time since March."

"That's not—"

"You skipped my wedding."

"I did not skip your wedding, my flight was—"

"I toasted you! I raised a genuine airport Bloody Mary in your honor."

"Guys. The letter."

"Ooh, milestones, that's good, that's a word."

"What milestones? Name three."

"Your wedding."

"Which you attended by phone—"

"We're calling it a promotion."

"It's not a promotion, it's a chair."

"'Marcus ascended to a leadership role,' how's that."

"That's a lie in ink."

"It's a Christmas letter, the whole genre is a lie in ink."

"Okay, okay. Three. What's three?"

"...The dog."

"The dog is not a milestone."

"We got a dog!"

"You got a dog. I got nothing. My year was flat."

"Denise, you climbed a mountain."

"A hill."

"You posted forty photos."

"It was a big hill."

"I live in Ohio."

"There are no—okay, I see what you did."
