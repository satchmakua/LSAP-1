Mar 3. Overcast. 41°F. Water level low. Reed stubble from last year, brown, standing. No song. Two mallards, north pool. Ice in shaded ditch, thin. City hum from the west, constant. Boots: mud line at ankle.

Mar 6. Clear. 38°F, rising to 50 by noon. Red-winged blackbird, one male, cattail at east berm. Conk-la-ree, three times. Epaulets bright. Killdeer over the gravel lot, calling. Frog: none heard. Water level unchanged. Willow tips reddening.

Mar 9. Rain overnight, 0.4 in. Water up two inches on the gauge. Blackbirds: four males now, spread along east and south. No females. Canada geese, pair, north pool, one honking. Skunk cabbage in the seep, three hoods, purple-green. Litter: one plastic bag, southwest fence. Removed.

Mar 12. Foggy a.m., clearing. 45°F. First chorus frog. Spring peepers, faint, west woods edge, single voice near dusk. Blackbird count: seven males. Grackle, one, iridescent, on the sign. Muskrat wake in the channel. Reed shoots not yet visible.

Mar 15. Wind SW, gusty. 52°F. Peepers louder, estimate a dozen, mostly after 6 p.m. Wood frog, one clack, gone. Song sparrow singing, cottonwood. Blackbirds displaying, tails spread, wings down. Turtle: painted, one, on the old plank, first of year, 2 p.m. sun. Cattail cotton blowing.

Mar 18. Cold snap. 33°F dawn. Frost on the boardwalk. Frogs silent. Blackbirds hunched, quiet by 9. Two herring gulls over. Water level down slightly, evaporation? Gauge reads 14. Feathers, gray, in the reeds—no body. Fox scat on the path, contains fur and one blackbird epaulet, red.

Mar 21. Equinox. Clear, 55°F. Peepers in full: estimated 40+, continuous after dark, ears ringing on the walk back. Chorus frogs, that thumb-on-comb sound, twenty or more. Wood ducks, pair, dropping into the flooded timber. Blackbird females arriving: three, brown, streaked. Skunk cabbage fully open. Willows gold-green haze.

Mar 24. Drizzle. 48°F. First reed spike, phragmites, no—native cattail, checked base, three inches, bright green, south pool edge. Marked with stake #1. Green heron: no. Great blue heron, one, standing north pool, twenty minutes, took one strike, swallowed. Bag of trash: none. Volunteer group planted 200 sedge plugs, east flat.

Mar 27. Warm. 61°F. Peepers all afternoon now, not just dusk. Toads: first American toad trill, long, from the culvert, one. Blackbirds nesting behavior, female weaving, cattail clump #3. Male chasing off a grackle. Cattail shoots at stake #1 now six inches. Second stake set, north cove. Dragonfly: no. Water striders, many, calm shallows.

Mar 30. Rain, steady, 0.9 in. Water up four inches. Sedge plugs holding. Frogs quiet in the rain. One heron print in mud, four-toe, 6 in. Bag: two cans, one bottle, removed. Tree swallows: four, over open water, first of year, low, catching something small. Boxes checked, one occupied? Straw in box 2.

Apr 2. Clearing after. 57°F. Chorus enormous—peepers, chorus frogs, one toad line droning under it all. Leopard frog seen, green, at ditch, jumped. Blackbird eggs: not checked, will not disturb. Swallows at box 2 and box 5. Cattail at stake #1: ten inches. Reeds greening in bulk now, whole east bank shifting color. Redwing count uncountable, dozens.

Apr 5. Windy, 50°F. Cold front. Frogs reduced, peepers only, patchy. Osprey overhead, circling, did not dive here. Yellow-rumped warblers, willows, six or more, flitting, tail flashes. Palm warbler, one, pumping tail on the ground. Muskrat lodge visible, new, north cove, fresh cut reed on top. Green: everywhere now.

Apr 8. Warm, still. 64°F. Toads breeding, culvert pool, three males calling, one pair in amplexus, spawn strings visible, black beads in double rows, several feet. Peepers waning as toads rise. Marsh marigold flowering, seep, yellow. First mosquito. Swallows: eight. Painted turtles: five on the plank. Snapping turtle, one, cruising, head up, north pool.

Apr 11. Rain a.m., sun p.m. 60°F. Toad spawn hatching—no, too soon. Egg strings intact. Sora: heard, whinny call, cattail, invisible, twice. First of year. Virginia rail: grunting series, south, unseen. Cattail at stake #1: fifteen inches, leaves broad. Warblers moving through: yellow, yellowthroat (masked male, singing witchety), a redstart. Water level 20.

Apr 14. Clear, 68°F. Marsh loud, layered: redwings, yellowthroat, sora, distant toads. Tadpoles in the culvert pool, small, black, schooling in the warm shallow, thousands. Damselflies emerging, blue, on the reeds, wings still soft. Swallows feeding young? Adults in and out of box 2, frequent. Snapping turtle laid eggs, gravel lot edge, hole and disturbed soil, tracks. Marked, roped off.

Apr 17. Overcast, muggy. 66°F. First green frog note—that loose banjo string, one, ditch. Leopard frogs several, grass edges, jumping ahead of boots. Reed canopy closing on east bank, three feet in places. Dragonflies: common whitetail, first, patrolling. Sedge plugs greening, roughly 180 of 200 alive, counted. Muskrat with young, two, trailing, channel. City skyline visible over the new leaf-line, hazed.

Apr 20. Sun, breezy. 70°F. Toad tadpoles dense, culvert, water warm to the hand. Frogs: green frogs up to four calling, spread. Peepers nearly done, one lone caller at dusk, stubborn. Blackbird fledglings? Begging calls, low in cattail #3. Warbler wave: magnolia, chestnut-sided, blackpoll, redstart, all in one willow, five minutes, gone. Turtles: seven on plank. Trash: none today.

Apr 23. Rain, 0.6 in. Water up. Cattail at stake #1: over two feet, first flower spike forming, brown pencil. Reeds now taller than the gauge post. Rails calling both species, dawn. Sora crossed the open channel, running on lily pads, seen clearly, brief. Bittern: possible, pumping call heard far north, three notes, low, unconfirmed. Swallows: many, whole colony working.

Apr 26. Clear, 74°F. Frogs: green frogs common now, gulp gulp across the pools, count pointless. Bullfrog: one deep note, first, north pool, unmistakable. Tadpoles growing, some with hind buds. Damselflies and dragonflies thick over reeds. Duck brood: mallard, hen with nine ducklings, north pool, single file. Wood duck brood also, twelve, dropped from box or tree, tiny. Cattail flower spikes on many stalks.

Apr 29. Warm, humid. 76°F. Marsh full: reeds head-high in the dense stands, green wall along east berm, gauge post nearly hidden. Redwings, grackles, yellowthroats, song sparrows, rails, one bittern (confirmed today, seen flying low, neck out, into reeds), swallows over all of it. Frogs continuous from culvert to north pool. Turtle hatch: no, too early. Snapping turtle nest intact, roped. Stake #1 cattail: chest high, four flower spikes. Water level 24, highest of season.

May 2. Clear, 78°F. Last entry, spring survey. Counted, standing on the berm at 7 a.m.: eleven singing redwing males in view. Two yellowthroats. Two rail species calling. One sora crossing. One bittern booming, north. Green frogs, uncounted. One bullfrog. Toad tadpoles, culvert, developing legs. Nine mallard ducklings, now larger, seven remaining. Cattail from bare stubble in March to a wall taller than me. Reeds where there was mud. Birds where there was hum. City still to the west, still humming, unchanged. The rest of it changed. Boots off at the car. Notebook closed.
